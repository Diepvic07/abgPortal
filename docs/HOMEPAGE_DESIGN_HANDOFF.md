# Homepage Design Handoff

## Purpose

This document defines the recommended homepage direction for the ABG Alumni portal.

The homepage should no longer behave like a news-first site. The primary job of the homepage is to communicate the core value of the product:

- this is an exclusive alumni network
- members can access a private database of 500+ alumni
- the platform helps members connect with each other
- access is gated and valuable

News should remain part of the experience, but not as the first or most dominant thing users see.

## Product Problem

Current user feedback suggests the homepage feels like a news site rather than a member network.

Translated test-user feedback:

`The current homepage is a news page, and that does not feel right. As a new person coming into ABG, I would look at this page and think the website has nothing special. I would not realize that it includes a feature to connect with other members. As a new member, I would want to understand what ABG Alumni is, what activities it has, and how to join, but I do not see a page explaining ABG Alumni.`

This feedback is valid.

If the product goals are:

- drive alumni sign-up
- highlight the exclusive member database
- convert members into paid users

then the homepage must sell the network first, and use news only as supporting proof of community activity.

## Homepage Strategy

Use a network-first homepage.

The first screen must immediately answer:

1. What is this platform?
2. Why is it valuable?
3. What should the user do next?

The homepage should be designed around the value proposition of exclusive access, trusted community, and connection opportunities.

## Positioning

### Primary value proposition

ABG Alumni is a private alumni network that gives members access to an exclusive database of 500+ alumni and helps them build meaningful connections across the community.

### Homepage priority order

1. Explain the network
2. Show why access is valuable
3. Push the user toward joining or requesting access
4. Build trust with proof and community signals
5. Show news and updates as supporting content

## Recommended Hero Section

### Headline

`Unlock Access to ABG's Exclusive 500+ Alumni Network`

### Subheadline

`Join a private member network built to help ABG alumni connect, discover opportunities, and stay engaged with the community.`

### Primary CTA

`Unlock Member Access`

### Secondary CTA

`Learn About ABG Alumni`

### Hero support bullets

Use 3 short supporting points directly under the hero copy:

- `Access a trusted network of 500+ alumni`
- `Find members across industries, cities, and generations`
- `Stay connected through community activities and updates`

## Section-by-Section Spec

### 1. Hero Section

Goal:

- communicate exclusivity and value immediately
- make the member network feel premium and useful
- drive the user to request or unlock access

Content:

- headline
- subheadline
- primary CTA
- secondary CTA
- 3 support bullets

Visual direction:

- premium and confident
- less like a blog header, more like a private community product
- hero should feel like the front door to a gated network

### 2. What ABG Alumni Is

Goal:

- help new visitors understand the organization and the purpose of the portal

Suggested copy direction:

`ABG Alumni brings members of the ABG community together through a private network designed for connection, support, and shared opportunities.`

`The portal gives alumni a trusted place to discover fellow members, stay close to community activity, and unlock the value of the ABG network.`

Include:

- short explanation of ABG Alumni
- short explanation of what the portal is for
- one short link or CTA to a deeper About page if available

### 3. Why Join the Network

Goal:

- explain the benefits of joining in concrete terms

Recommended content blocks:

- `Connect with alumni`
- `Discover opportunities`
- `Stay engaged with the community`

Suggested copy:

`Access a curated alumni directory, request introductions, and build relationships inside a trusted private community.`

`Use the network to reconnect, explore career opportunities, and stay informed about what is happening across ABG Alumni.`

### 4. What You Can Do on the Portal

Goal:

- explain the core product features clearly
- make the portal feel like a useful member tool, not just a brand page
- connect the homepage message to the actual member experience

Recommended feature blocks:

- `Search the alumni network`
- `Request introductions`
- `Follow community updates`
- `Unlock premium access`

Suggested copy direction:

`Use the portal to explore the alumni directory, discover members across industries and locations, and request meaningful connections inside the ABG network.`

`Stay updated on alumni activities and unlock full member access for deeper discovery and premium features.`

Feature guidance:

- `Search the alumni network`
  - explain that members can discover alumni across classes, industries, cities, and countries
- `Request introductions`
  - explain that the portal helps members reach out and connect with other alumni
- `Follow community updates`
  - explain that members can keep up with news, activities, and community momentum
- `Unlock premium access`
  - explain that the premium layer unlocks the full value of the network

This section should make the product feature set explicit for first-time visitors.

### 5. How It Works

Goal:

- make the journey feel simple and trustworthy

Recommended steps:

1. `Sign up with your current email`
2. `Claim your alumni profile or request review`
3. `Get approved and unlock the member network`

Optional fourth step if wanted:

4. `Upgrade for full access and premium features`

This section should reduce confusion about access and verification.

### 6. Social Proof and Community Stats

Goal:

- make the network feel real, active, and valuable

Suggested stats:

- `500+ alumni profiles`
- countries represented
- industries represented
- community activities hosted

If exact numbers are not available yet, do not invent them. Use only real numbers.

### 7. Latest News and Activities

Goal:

- show that the community is active
- support credibility without taking over the homepage

Guidelines:

- show only a small preview of recent news
- use this as a supporting section, not the hero
- include a CTA such as `View All Updates`

Recommended structure:

- section heading
- 3 latest items
- optional event/news thumbnails
- link to full news page

### 8. Final CTA

Goal:

- close the page with a strong conversion action

Recommended final CTA copy:

Headline:

`Ready to unlock the ABG alumni network?`

Button:

`Unlock Member Access`

Support text:

`Join the private ABG community and start building meaningful connections.`

## CTA Guidance

### Primary CTA usage

Use `Unlock Member Access` consistently in the homepage hero and final CTA.

This CTA is better than `Sign Up` because:

- it feels more premium
- it reflects the gated approval model
- it signals that access itself has value

### Secondary CTA usage

Use `Learn About ABG Alumni` as the secondary CTA for first-time visitors who need context before joining.

This is important because new visitors may not yet understand what ABG Alumni is.

## News Positioning Rule

News should not be the first thing a visitor sees on the homepage.

News should serve these roles:

- proof that the community is active
- ongoing engagement for existing members
- supporting content for trust and energy

News should not define the homepage identity.

The homepage identity should be:

- exclusive alumni network
- member connection portal
- premium community access point

## Audience Considerations

The homepage must work for two visitor mindsets:

### New visitor

Needs:

- understand what ABG Alumni is
- understand why the portal matters
- understand how to join

### Existing or returning member

Needs:

- quick reassurance that this is the alumni network
- easy path to access the directory
- confidence that the platform is active and valuable

The chosen structure supports both audiences without letting news dominate.

## Design Direction

Recommended tone:

- premium
- warm
- trusted
- exclusive but welcoming

Avoid:

- making the homepage look like a generic blog
- making news cards visually dominate the hero
- unclear generic CTAs like `Read More` or `Explore`

Prefer:

- strong headline
- obvious gated-access CTA
- visible trust signals
- concise, benefit-driven copy

## Functional Requirements

The homepage should include:

- hero section with primary and secondary CTA
- section introducing ABG Alumni
- benefits section
- feature section explaining what members can do on the portal
- simple onboarding/how-it-works section
- stats/social proof section
- latest news preview section
- final CTA section

The homepage should support:

- desktop and mobile layouts
- clear CTA visibility above the fold
- fast loading

## Content Requirements

Required homepage copy at minimum:

- hero headline
- hero subheadline
- primary CTA label
- secondary CTA label
- short ABG Alumni introduction
- 3 benefit statements
- 4 product feature statements
- 3-step how-it-works copy
- final CTA block

Optional additions:

- member testimonial
- featured alumni spotlight
- featured community event

## Success Criteria

The new homepage should make a first-time visitor understand all of the following within a few seconds:

1. This is ABG Alumni
2. This is a private alumni network, not just a news site
3. The core value is access to an exclusive alumni database and community
4. The next action is to request or unlock access

## Acceptance Criteria

1. The hero communicates the alumni-network value proposition before any news content appears.
2. The primary CTA is `Unlock Member Access`.
3. The secondary CTA is `Learn About ABG Alumni`.
4. The page includes a section explaining what users can do on the portal.
5. News appears lower on the homepage as a supporting section.
6. The page explains what ABG Alumni is for first-time visitors.
7. The page makes the member database and connection value visible.
8. The design supports both conversion and community storytelling.

## Recommended Next Step for Development

Implement the homepage as a new landing experience that sits above the news feed rather than reusing the news layout as the homepage.

If the product later needs a dedicated news page, keep it separate from the main landing page.
