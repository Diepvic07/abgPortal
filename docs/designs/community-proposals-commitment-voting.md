# Design: Community Proposals with Commitment-Weighted Voting

## Problem Statement

ABG Alumni Connect has 500+ members, 100 active. 20% regularly propose community activity ideas directly to the Board through informal channels (chat, DMs). These ideas are not documented, not shared with other members, and there's no structured way to gauge community support or commitment. Good ideas get lost. The Board has no ranked signal showing which ideas have real backing.

## Demand Evidence

- 20% of 500+ members actively propose ideas to the Board already
- Ideas flow through informal channels but are not documented or shared broadly
- The Board needs a ranked signal to prioritize which ideas to act on
- This formalizes existing behavior, not creating behavior from scratch

## Status Quo

Members message the Board directly with activity ideas. The Board discusses internally, makes decisions without community-wide input, and announces. Other members don't see what ideas exist, can't express support, and can't commit to helping. The Board has no way to distinguish "everyone likes this" from "5 people will actually show up and do the work."

## Target User and Narrowest Wedge

**Primary user:** Active ABG members who want to propose and execute community activities (charity events, learning sessions, community support initiatives).

**Narrowest wedge:** A proposal board where proposers commit as taskforce leaders and supporters declare their participation level (1-10). Ideas ranked by total committed participation, not popularity.

## Constraints

- Existing stack: Next.js 16, Supabase, Vercel, Tailwind CSS 4
- Must integrate with existing auth (NextAuth, Google OAuth + magic link)
- Must work with existing member approval flow (approval_status = approved)
- Vietnamese alumni community, verified members, high-trust environment
- Admin tools must be part of the existing /admin section
- i18n required (Vietnamese + English)
- PostHog analytics integration

## Premises

1. Members already share ideas (20% of 500+), this formalizes existing behavior
2. The right signal is commitment, not popularity. Voters declare participation level (1-10), proposals rank by total committed effort
3. Proposers must commit to leading the taskforce (skin in the game)
4. Open to ALL approved members, not just premium. This feature IS the value proposition of ABG
5. Immediate publication with admin moderation (edit, remove, merge). Verified community, high trust

## Cross-Model Perspective

Independent Claude subagent review (cold read, no conversation context):

**Steelman:** ABG is building a commitment marketplace for community action. Ideas compete on pledged effort, not likes. Proposers lead, voters stake participation. The public page turns internal energy into a recruiting tool.

**Key insight:** The real problem isn't idea generation (20% already propose). It's selection and execution. Most community platforms die from "lots of likes, nobody shows up." Commitment-weighted voting collapses the gap between "I support this" and "I'll do something about it."

**Challenged premise:** Immediate publication may cause proposal flooding with 100 active members. Suggested co-sponsor requirement or cooldown. User chose to keep immediate publication with admin moderation powers as the gate.

**Prototype recommendation:** 3 tables (proposals, commitments, comments), 3+ pages. Test one hypothesis: will members commit when commitment is visible and quantified?

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Minimal Viable, "Commitment Board"
Simple proposal + commitment voting (1-10). Ranked by sum of commitment scores. No taskforce tracking post-selection.
- Effort: M (human ~1 week / CC ~30 min)
- Risk: Low
- Completeness: 6/10

### Approach B: Full Commitment System, "Proposal + Taskforce" (CHOSEN)
Proposals + commitment voting + taskforce dashboard + admin accountability tracking + public page. Full lifecycle: propose, commit, execute, deliver.
- Effort: L (human ~2 weeks / CC ~45 min initial scaffolding, plus integration testing and polish)
- Risk: Medium
- Completeness: 9/10

### Approach C: Lightweight + Viral, "Pledge Board"
Binary "I'm in" pledges. Public-facing page. No granularity.
- Effort: S (human ~3 days / CC ~20 min)
- Risk: Low
- Completeness: 4/10

## Recommended Approach

**Approach B: Full Commitment System** because:
- 20% of members already propose ideas. This is formalizing existing behavior, not validating a hypothesis.
- The taskforce tracking and accountability loop is what makes this different from every other idea board.
- With CC, the delta between A and B is ~15 minutes of implementation time.
- The public page (from C) is included for member recruitment.

### Core Features

1. **Proposal Creation** (all approved members)
   - Title, description, category (charity, event, learning, community_support, other)
   - Proposer auto-commits as taskforce lead at commitment level 10
   - Immediate publication, no admin pre-approval

2. **Commitment Voting** (all approved members)
   - Participation level slider: 1-10 scale with labels:
     - 1-2: "Moral support" (interested, will follow along)
     - 3-4: "Will attend" (show up to meetings/events)
     - 5-6: "Active participant" (will contribute time regularly)
     - 7-8: "Core contributor" (significant time commitment)
     - 9-10: "Will lead a workstream" (take ownership of a piece)
   - One commitment per member per proposal (can update, can remove)
   - Proposals ranked by SUM(commitment_level) descending

3. **Taskforce Dashboard** (visible to all members for selected proposals)
   - List of committed members + their commitment levels
   - Proposer marked as lead
   - After completion: admin enters actual participation score (1-10) per member
   - Comparison view: committed level vs actual participation per member

4. **Admin Tools**
   - Edit, remove proposals
   - Merge proposals: surviving proposal inherits all commitments and comments from merged proposal; merged proposal gets status `merged` with a reference to the surviving proposal
   - Change proposal status (see state machine below)
   - Pin proposals
   - Track actual participation after completion (1-10 per member)
   - Reassign taskforce lead if original proposer becomes inactive
   - View commitment analytics

5. **Public Summary Page** (no login required)
   - Active proposals with commitment scores
   - "Join ABG to participate" CTA
   - Showcases what ABG is building to attract new members

6. **Comments** (all approved members)
   - Named comments under proposals
   - Author can edit/delete own comments
   - Admin can moderate (hidden = temporarily hidden by admin, can be restored; removed = soft delete, preserved in DB for audit but not shown to members)

7. **Notifications** (email via Resend)
   - Proposer gets email when someone commits to their proposal
   - Members who committed get email when proposal status changes
   - Defer in-app notifications to a later phase

### Proposal Status State Machine

```
  published ──► selected ──► in_progress ──► completed
      │              │             │
      ▼              ▼             ▼
  archived       published     published (revert)
      │          (revert)
      ▼
  removed (admin only, terminal)
  merged (admin only, terminal, references surviving proposal)
```

- Only admins can change status
- `archived`: proposer can self-archive if commitment_count = 0 (exception to admin-only rule); admin can archive anytime
- `removed` and `merged` are terminal states (no transitions out)
- Reverting from `selected` or `in_progress` back to `published` re-opens commitment voting
- `removed` = soft delete (status change, row preserved for audit), NOT hard delete

### Input Validation

- Title: 5-200 characters
- Description: 20-5000 characters
- Comment body: 1-2000 characters
- Category: must be one of the enum values

### Pagination & Filtering

- GET /api/community/proposals supports: `?page=1&limit=20&category=charity&status=published&sort=commitment_score`
- Default: page 1, limit 20, all categories, status = published, sort = commitment_score desc
- Pinned proposals always appear first regardless of sort

### Merge Conflict Resolution

When merging proposal B into proposal A:
- If a member committed to both, keep the higher commitment level
- All comments from B are moved to A with a system note "[Merged from: original title]"
- Merge operation bypasses triggers and does a manual recount of commitment_score, commitment_count, and comment_count on the surviving proposal

### Proposer Commitment Rules

- The proposer's auto-commitment (level 10) is a regular row in `community_commitments`
- Proposer can lower their commitment level but cannot remove it while proposal status is `published`, `selected`, or `in_progress`
- Proposer can only remove their commitment if proposal is `archived` (effectively abandoning it)

### Actual Participation Tracking

- Admin can only set `actual_participation` on commitments when proposal status = `completed`
- Score is 1-10, same scale as commitment level
- Not shown publicly, only visible to admins and the individual member

### Data Model

**New tables:**

`community_proposals`
- id, created_by_member_id, title, description, category, status
- is_pinned, commitment_score (denormalized SUM), commitment_count
- comment_count, target_date (optional), created_at, updated_at, published_at
- selected_at, selected_by_member_id, completed_at, admin_note
- merged_into_id (nullable, references surviving proposal when status = merged)

`community_commitments`
- id, proposal_id, member_id, commitment_level (1-10)
- created_at, updated_at
- actual_participation (nullable, filled by admin post-completion)
- UNIQUE(proposal_id, member_id)

`community_proposal_comments`
- id, proposal_id, member_id, body, status (visible/hidden/removed)
- created_at, updated_at

### Denormalized Field Sync

`commitment_score`, `commitment_count`, and `comment_count` on `community_proposals` are kept in sync via Postgres triggers on INSERT/UPDATE/DELETE of `community_commitments` and `community_proposal_comments`. This ensures data integrity regardless of whether changes come from the API or direct DB operations.

### RLS Policies

- `community_proposals`: all approved members can SELECT published proposals; only author can UPDATE own proposal (when commitment_count = 0); admins can UPDATE any proposal
- `community_commitments`: all approved members can SELECT; authenticated members can INSERT/UPDATE/DELETE own commitments
- `community_proposal_comments`: all approved members can SELECT visible comments; authenticated members can INSERT and UPDATE/DELETE own comments; admins can UPDATE status on any comment

### API Routes

**Member routes (auth required, approved members):**
- GET /api/community/proposals (list, sorted by commitment_score)
- POST /api/community/proposals (create)
- GET /api/community/proposals/[id] (detail + commitments + comments)
- PATCH /api/community/proposals/[id] (edit own, only when commitment_count = 0)
- POST /api/community/proposals/[id]/commit (upsert commitment with level 1-10)
- DELETE /api/community/proposals/[id]/commit (remove own commitment)
- GET /api/community/proposals/[id]/comments (list comments)
- POST /api/community/proposals/[id]/comments (add comment)
- PATCH /api/community/proposals/[id]/comments/[comment_id] (edit own comment)
- DELETE /api/community/proposals/[id]/comments/[comment_id] (delete own comment)

**Public route (no auth):**
- GET /api/community/proposals/public (summary for public page)

**Admin routes:**
- PATCH /api/admin/community/proposals/[id]/status (change status)
- PATCH /api/admin/community/proposals/[id]/pin (toggle pin)
- PATCH /api/admin/community/proposals/[id]/edit (admin edit title/description/category)
- POST /api/admin/community/proposals/[id]/merge (merge into another proposal)
- PATCH /api/admin/community/proposals/[id]/lead (reassign taskforce lead)
- PATCH /api/admin/community/commitments/[id]/actual (record actual participation)
- PATCH /api/admin/community/proposals/[id]/remove (soft delete, sets status = removed)
- PATCH /api/admin/community/comments/[id]/moderate (hide/remove comment)

### Page Routes

- /proposals (list page, requires auth + approved)
- /proposals/new (create form, requires auth + approved)
- /proposals/[id] (detail page with commitments + comments, requires auth + approved)
- /community (public summary page, no auth required, standalone layout — shows published + selected + in_progress proposals, titles + descriptions + commitment counts, no member names)

### Ranking Algorithm

```
proposal_score = SUM(commitment_level) for all commitments on that proposal
```

Default sort: highest score first. Pinned proposals appear at top regardless of score.
