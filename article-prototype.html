Member Spotlight: How John found his technical co-founder in 48 hours

Finding the right technical co-founder is often cited as the hardest part of building a tech startup. But for John D., an ABG Alumni, it only took 48 hours using our precise, noise-free matching algorithm.

John had been looking for a CTO for 6 months using standard job boards, LinkedIn Outreach, and Facebook groups. "The problem wasn't a lack of engineers," John explained. "The problem was finding an engineer who shared the ABG core values and had the specific fintech background I needed. My inbox was full of noise."

The Process

Last Tuesday, John decided to try the new ABG Alumni Connect platform. He selected the "Partner Matching" category and typed a simple request:

"I am a non-technical founder building a B2B payments platform. I have secured pre-seed funding and need a technical co-founder (CTO) with Node.js experience and a deep understanding of Vietnamese payment gateways."

Within seconds, the AI returned 5 highly relevant profiles. While the names and current companies were masked to protect privacy, the "Reason for Match" generated by our AI was exceptionally clear.

The Connection

John saw a profile that perfectly matched his needs and clicked 'Connect'. Because he was a Pro member, he drafted a custom introductory note.

  • Tuesday 2:00 PM: John sends the connection request.
  • Tuesday 4:30 PM: Sarah (the masked CTO) reads the anonymous request, sees the business proposition, and clicks "Accept."
  • Wednesday: Sarah and John exchange emails via the automated introduction thread.
  • Thursday 9:00 AM: They meet for coffee. By Friday, they shook hands on a partnership.

Why it Worked

By restricting the pool to vetted ABG Alumni, the foundational trust is already established. The AI simply acts as a hyper-efficient router, ensuring that busy professionals only see requests that are highly relevant to their skills and interests.
